Analog vs. Digital (Saturation)—
The audio data we're reading in is digital, or has discrete values, since computers store information in bits (0 and 1s). However, actually sound is continuous and so when we move from actual sound (a physical process— analog) to a digital process, we have to take into account frame rate— the more frame rates, the less distinction one can make between continuous actual sound and digital discrete values.
